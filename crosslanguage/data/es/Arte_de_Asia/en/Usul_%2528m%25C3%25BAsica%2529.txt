The usul in Ottoman classical music is an underlying rhythmic cycle that complements the melodic rhythm and sometimes helping to shape the overall structure of a musical piece. == Description == A usul can be as short as two or as long as pulses 128 pulses. Usul usually translated as "metro", but usul and subway are not exactly the same. Both are repeating rhythmic patterns with more or less complex structures of the pulses of variable duration and weight. However, a student learning Turkish music in the traditional system memorises the first mesk usul kinetically banging knees with hands. The student then sings the vocal or instrumental composition, as interpreted by the underlying usul. This pedagogical system helps students to memorize the composition, while the rhythmic structure assimilates subyacente.Los usul standard patterns are represented by words built from combinations of syllables düm, dü-um, tek, tekkyaa, teke, te-ek where düm, dü-um indicate a strong pace on single or double length; and tek, tekkya, teke, te-ek indicate various combinations of light pulses on average, single or double length. Long usul (eg 28/4, 32/4, 120/4) are composite structures underlying metric longest compositions enteras.En Ottoman times the usul sections was played by percussionists. The drums are usually omitted in modern performances except Mevlevi.
