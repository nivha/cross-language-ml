300px | right | thumb | dungchen pair photographed in 1938 by an expedition alemanaEl dungchen (Tibetan: དུང་ཆེན) is a great trumpet used in the ceremonies of Tibetan Buddhism. It is possibly the most widely used in Buddhist culture Tibet.Se instrument is commonly used in pairs or larger groups, and their sound is often compared to the singing of elephants. Each of the horns has a conical copper tube divided into three sections which fit in the manner of a telescope.
