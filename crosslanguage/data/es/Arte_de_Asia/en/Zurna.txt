thumb | 130px | right | left Zurna Turkish, Syrian derecha.El the zurna, zourna, Zorna, zurla, zokra, Surnay, surnai, zamr, zamour or mizmar, is a wind instrument of the double reed big family the oboes. Its origins date back to the eighth century. It takes its name from the Persian زورنه (zur: party, banquet and ney: reed or tongue). In North Africa, is recognized under the following names: algaita, Ghaita, rajta, rhaita, etc. The zurna is extended by Muslim countries, notably Turkey, Iran, Syria, Iraq, Lebanon, Egypt , in North Africa, Niger, and in Azerbaijan, Armenia, Greece (also known as pipiza and karamouza) and the Balkans. An important variation is the suona, China.
