thumb | right | 150px | Persian Painting, 1669.El Kamanche, kemenche, Kamanche, Kamancha or kemence is a bowed string instrument originating in Persia. It is used in traditional classical music of Iran, Armenia, Azerbaijan, Uzbekistan, Turkmenistan and the region of the Black Sea, with slight variations in the structure of the instrument. Kashmir is known as "saz-i Kashmir" ("saz Kashmir '). Traditionally, kamanchí has three silk strings, but the modern has four metal strings, while the tuning pegs are made of ivory. It is played with a bow, which in the case of professional instruments are made with horsehair.
