Ahmed Ratip Canga (Istanbul, September 24, 1905 - Buenos Aires, December 13, 1993) was a Turkish jazz guitarist of Yemeni origin == History == At 19, his father sent him to study engineering at Chicago. ; began taking classes with banjo, but soon switched to electric guitar. Then returned to Turkey to do his military service. In 1933 he moved to Uruguay. He formed a group called "Ahmed Ratip and Cotton Pickers", of which he was director. Artistically he was known as Mike. He was one of the pioneers in Argentina in 1946 jazz.En interpret Tito Alberti joined his band, playing in a stable at the Teatro Colon. Ahmed died on December 13, 1993 and is buried in the cemetery of Chacarita cemetery in the Guild of Authors and Composers.
