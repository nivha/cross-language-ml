{"text": "The near-horizon metric (NHM) refers to the near-horizon limit of the global metric of a black hole. NHMs play an important role in studying the geometry and topology of black holes, but are only well defined for extremal black holes. NHMs are expressed in Gaussian null coordinates, and one important property is that the dependence on the coordinate r is fixed in the near-horizon limit.\n\n==NHM of extremal Reissner\u2013Nordstr\u00f6m black holes==\n\nThe metric of extremal Reissner\u2013Nordstr\u00f6m black hole  is\n\n:ds^2\\,=\\,-\\Big(1-\\frac{M}{r}\\Big)^2\\,dt^2+\\Big(1-\\frac{M}{r}\\Big)^{-2}dr^2+r^2\\,\\big(d\\theta^2+\\sin^2\\theta\\,d\\phi^2 \\big)\\,.\n\nTaking the near-horizon limit\n\n:t\\mapsto \\frac{\\tilde{t}}{\\epsilon}\\,,\\quad r\\mapsto M+\\epsilon\\,\\tilde{r}\\,,\\quad \\epsilon\\to 0\\,,\n\nand then omitting the tildes, one obtains the near-horizon metric\n\n:ds^2=-\\frac{r^2}{M^2}\\,dt^2+\\frac{M^2}{r^2}\\,dr^2+M^2\\,\\big(d\\theta^2+\\sin^2\\theta\\,d\\phi^2 \\big)\n\n==NHM of extremal Kerr black holes ==\n\nThe metric of extremal Kerr black hole (M=a=J/M) in Boyer\u2013Lindquist coordinates can be written in the following two enlightening forms,\n\n:ds^2\\,=\\,-\\frac{\\rho_K^2\\Delta_K}{\\Sigma^2}\\,dt^2+\\frac{\\rho_K^2}{\\Delta_K}\\,dr^2+\\rho_K^2d\\theta^2+\\frac{\\Sigma^2\\sin^2\\theta}{\\rho_K^2}\\big( d\\phi-\\omega_K\\, dt \\big)^2\\,,\n\n:ds^2\\,=\\,-\\frac{\\Delta_K}{\\rho_K^2}\\,\\big(dt-M\\sin^2\\theta d\\phi \\big)^2+\\frac{\\rho_K^2}{\\Delta_K}\\,dr^2+\\rho_K^2 d\\theta^2+\\frac{\\sin^2\\theta}{\\rho_K^2}\\Big( Mdt-(r^2+M^2)d\\phi \\Big)^2\\,,\n\nwhere\n\n:\\rho_K^2:=r^2+M^2\\cos^2\\theta\\,,\\;\\;  \\Delta_K:=\\big(r-M\\big)^2\\,,\\;\\;  \\Sigma^2:=\\big(r+M^2\\big)^2-M^2\\Delta_K\\sin^2\\theta\\,,\\;\\;  \\omega_K:=\\frac{2M^2 r}{\\Sigma^2}\\,.\n\nTaking the  near-horizon limitJames Bardeen, Gary T Horowitz.
