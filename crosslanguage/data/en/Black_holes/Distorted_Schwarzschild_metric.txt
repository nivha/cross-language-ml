{"text": "The distorted Schwarzschild metric refers to the metric of a standard/isolated  Schwarzschild spacetime exposed in external fields. In numerical simulation, the Schwarzschild metric can be distorted by almost arbitrary kinds of external energy\u2013momentum distribution. However, in exact analysis, the mature method to distort the standard Schwarzschild metric is restricted to the framework of Weyl metrics.\n\n==Standard Schwarzschild as a vacuum Weyl metric==\n\nAll static axisymmetric solutions of the Einstein-Maxwell equations can be written in the form of Weyl's metric,\n\n(1)\\quad ds^2=-e^{2\\psi(\\rho,z)}dt^2+e^{2\\gamma(\\rho,z)-2\\psi(\\rho,z)}(d\\rho^2+dz^2)+e^{-2\\psi(\\rho,z)}\\rho^2 d\\phi^2\\,,\n\nFrom the Weyl perspective, the metric potentials generating the standard Schwarzschild solution are given by\n\n(2)\\quad  \\psi_{SS}=\\frac{1}{2}\\ln\\frac{L-M}{L+M}\\,,\\quad \\gamma_{SS}=\\frac{1}{2}\\ln\\frac{L^2-M^2}{l_+  l_-}\\,,\n\nwhere\n\n(3)\\quad  L=\\frac{1}{2}\\big(l_+ + l_- \\big)\\,,\\quad l_+ =\\sqrt{\\rho^2+(z+M)^2}\\,,\\quad l_- =\\sqrt{\\rho^2+(z-M)^2}\\,,\n\nwhich yields the Schwarzschild metric in Weyl's canonical coordinates that\n\n(4)\\quad ds^2=-\\frac{L-M}{L+M}dt^2+\\frac{(L+M)^2}{l_+  l_-}(d\\rho^2+dz^2)+\\frac{L+M}{L-M}\\,\\rho^2 d\\phi^2\\,.\n\n==Weyl-distortion of Schwarzschild's metric==\n\nVacuum Weyl spacetimes (such as Schwarzschild) respect the following field equations,\n\n(5.a)\\quad \\nabla^2 \\psi =0\\,,\n(5.b)\\quad \\gamma_{,\\,\\rho}=\\rho\\,\\Big(\\psi^2_{,\\,\\rho}-\\psi^2_{,\\,z} \\Big)\\,,\n(5.c)\\quad \\gamma_{,\\,z}=2\\,\\rho\\,\\psi_{,\\,\\rho}\\psi_{,\\,z}\\,,\n(5.d)\\quad \\gamma_{,\\,\\rho\\rho}+\\gamma_{,\\,zz}=-\\big(\\psi^2_{,\\,\\rho}+\\psi^2_{,\\,z} \\big)\\,,\n\nwhere \\nabla^2:= \\partial_{\\rho\\rho}+\\frac{1}{\\rho}\\partial_\\rho +\\partial_{zz} is the Laplace operator.\n\nDerivation of vacuum field equations\n\nThe vacuum Einstein's equation reads R_{ab}=0, which yields Eqs(5.a)-(5.c).\n\nMoreover, the supplementary relation R=0 implies Eq(5.d).\n\nEq(5.a) is the linear Laplace's equation; that is to say, linear combinations of given solutions are still its solutions. Given two solutions \\{\\psi^{\\langle1\\rangle}, \\psi^{\\langle2\\rangle}\\} to Eq(5.a), one can construct a new solution via\n\n(6)\\quad \n\\tilde\\psi\\,=\\,\\psi^{\\langle1\\rangle}+\\psi^{\\langle2\\rangle}\\,,\n\nand the other metric potential can be obtained by\n\n(7)\\quad \n\\tilde\\gamma\\,=\\,\\gamma^{\\langle1\\rangle}+\\gamma^{\\langle2\\rangle}+2\\int\\rho\\,\\Big\\{\\,\\Big( \\psi^{\\langle1\\rangle}_{,\\,\\rho}\\psi^{\\langle2\\rangle}_{,\\,\\rho}-\\psi^{\\langle1\\rangle}_{,\\,z}\\psi^{\\langle2\\rangle}_{,\\,z} \\Big)\\,d\\rho +\\Big( \\psi^{\\langle1\\rangle}_{,\\,\\rho}\\psi^{\\langle2\\rangle}_{,\\,z}+\\psi^{\\langle1\\rangle}_{,\\,z}\\psi^{\\langle2\\rangle}_{,\\,\\rho} \\Big)\\,dz \\,  \\Big\\}\\,.\n\nLet \\psi^{\\langle1\\rangle}=\\psi_{SS} and \\gamma^{\\langle1\\rangle}=\\gamma_{SS}, while \\psi^{\\langle2\\rangle}=\\psi and \\gamma^{\\langle2\\rangle}=\\gamma refer to a second set of Weyl metric potentials. Then, \\{\\tilde\\psi, \\tilde\\gamma \\} constructed via  \nEqs(6)(7) leads to the superposed Schwarzschild-Weyl metric\n\n(8)\\quad \nds^2=-e^{2\\psi(\\rho,z)}\\frac{L-M}{L+M}dt^2+e^{2\\gamma(\\rho,z)-2\\psi(\\rho,z)}\\frac{(L+M)^2}{l_+  l_-}(d\\rho^2+dz^2)+e^{-2\\psi(\\rho,z)}\\frac{L+M}{L-M}\\,\\rho^2 d\\phi^2\\,.\n\nWith the transformations\n\n(9)\\quad L+M=r\\,,\\quad l_+ + l_- =2M\\cos\\theta\\,,\\quad z=(r-M)\\cos\\theta\\,,\n\\;\\;\\quad \\rho=\\sqrt{r^2-2Mr}\\,\\sin\\theta\\,,\\quad l_+  l_-=(r-M)^2-M^2\\cos^2\\theta\\,,\n\none can obtain the superposed Schwarzschild metric in the usual \\{t,r,\\theta,\\phi\\} coordinates,\n\n(10)\\quad \nds^2=-e^{2\\psi(r,\\theta)}\\,\\Big(1-\\frac{2M}{r} \\Big)\\,dt^2+e^{2\\gamma(r,\\theta)-2\\psi(r,\\theta)}\\Big\\{\\,\\Big(1-\\frac{2M}{r} \\Big)^{-1}dr^2+r^2d\\theta^2\\,\\Big\\}+e^{-2\\psi(r,\\theta)}r^2\\sin^2\\theta\\, d\\phi^2\\,.\n\nThe superposed metric Eq(10) can be regarded as the standard Schwarzschild metric distorted by external Weyl sources.
