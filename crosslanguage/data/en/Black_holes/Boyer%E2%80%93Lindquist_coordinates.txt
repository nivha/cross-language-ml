{"text": "In the mathematical description of general relativity, the Boyer\u2013Lindquist coordinates are a generalization of the coordinates used for the metric of a Schwarzschild black hole that can be used to express the metric of a Kerr black hole.\n\nThe coordinate transformation from Boyer\u2013Lindquist coordinates r, \\theta, \\phi to cartesian coordinates x, y, z is given by\n::{x} = \\sqrt {r^2 + a^2} \\sin\\theta\\cos\\phi\n::{y} = \\sqrt {r^2 + a^2} \\sin\\theta\\sin\\phi\n::{z} = r \\cos\\theta \\quad\n\nThe line element for a black hole with mass M, angular momentum J, and charge Q in Boyer\u2013Lindquist coordinates and natural units (G=c=1) is\n\n:: ds^2 = -\\frac{\\Delta}{\\Sigma}\\left(dt - a \\sin^2\\theta d\\phi \\right)^2 +\\frac{\\sin^2\\theta}{\\Sigma}\\Big((r^2+a^2)d\\phi - a dt\\Big)^2 + \\frac{\\Sigma}{\\Delta}dr^2 + \\Sigma d\\theta^2\n\nwhere\n\n:: \\Delta = r^2 - 2Mr + a^2 + Q^2 \n:: \\Sigma = r^2 + a^2 \\cos^2\\theta \n:: a = J/M , the angular momentum per unit mass of the black hole\n\nNote that in natural units M, a, and Q all have units of length.  This line element describes the Kerr\u2013Newman metric.\n\nThe Hamiltonian for test particle motion in Kerr spacetime was separable in Boyer\u2013Lindquist coordinates.
