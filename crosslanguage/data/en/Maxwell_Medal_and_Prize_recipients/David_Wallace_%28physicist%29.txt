'''Sir David James Wallace''', [[Order of the British Empire|CBE]], [[Royal Society|FRS]], [[Royal Academy of Engineering|FREng]], [[Deputy Lieutenant|DL]] (born 1945) is a physicist and  master of [[Churchill College, Cambridge]].<ref name="microsoft">{{AcademicSearch|42037095}}</ref>

==Education==
Wallace was educated at [[Hawick High School]]<ref name="whoswho"/> in [[Hawick]], [[Scottish Borders|Borders]], [[Scotland]] and went to the [[University of Edinburgh]] where he earned a degree in Mathematical Physics and a PhD in [[Elementary particle|Elementary Particle]] Theory, under the supervision of [[Peter Higgs]].<ref name="mathgene"/><ref name="wallacephd">{{cite thesis |degree=PhD |first=David|last=Wallace |title=Applications of Current Algebras and Chiral Symmetry Breaking |publisher=University of Edinburgh |date=1971 |url=http://books.google.co.uk/books?id=a64FcgAACAAJ|authorlink=David Wallace (physicist)}}</ref>

==Research==
After [[postdoctoral research]] work as a [[Harkness Fellowship|Harkness Fellow]] at [[Princeton University]], Wallace became a physics lecturer at the [[University of Southampton]] in 1972.

In 1979, he became [[Peter Guthrie Tait|Tait]] Professor of Mathematical Physics at the [[University of Edinburgh]]. He became Director of the [[EPCC|Edinburgh Parallel Computing Centre (EPCC)]] and in 1996 he was awarded the CBE for his computing work.

He was formerly Vice President and Treasurer of the [[Royal Society]] and Chair of the [[Council for the Mathematical Sciences]]. From 1994 to January 2006 he was the [[Vice-Chancellor]] of [[Loughborough University]]. From 2006 to 2011 he was the Director of the [[Isaac Newton Institute for Mathematical Sciences]] in [[University of Cambridge|Cambridge]]. Wallace has also been President of the [[Institute of Physics]] and [[Deputy Lieutenant]] of [[Leicestershire]].

==Personal==
He has a wife, Elizabeth<ref name="whoswho"/><ref name="cv">{{cite web |url=http://www.chu.cam.ac.uk/about/fellows/DavidWallaceCVSeptember2012.pdf |title=David Wallace CV |format= |work= |archivedate=2013-02-12|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/6ENhfbrPa}}</ref> and a daughter, Sara. He enjoys [[Edible mushroom|mycophagy]].
