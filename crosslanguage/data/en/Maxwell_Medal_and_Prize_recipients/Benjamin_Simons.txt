'''Ben Simons''' is a [[People of the United Kingdom|British]] [[theoretical physics|theoretical physicist]], working in the field of theoretical [[condensed matter physics]] and in [[biophysics]].

Simons is Professor of Theoretical Physics at the [[University of Cambridge]] [[Cavendish Laboratory]].<ref name="aps"/> In 2001 he was awarded the [[Maxwell Medal and Prize]] by the [[Institute of Physics]].<ref name=iop-maxwell>{{cite web|title=Maxwell medal recipients|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/career/maxwell/medallists/page_38660.html|publisher=Institute of Physics|accessdate=25 April 2011}}</ref>

Simons has two brothers, Thomas and Joseph. His father is a professor at Oxford University, Professor John. P Simons. He is married and has two children.
