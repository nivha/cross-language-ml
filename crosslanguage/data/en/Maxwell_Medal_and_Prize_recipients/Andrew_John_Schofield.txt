'''Andrew John Schofield''' (Andy Schofield) is a [[theoretical physicist]] and a [[professor]] at the [[University of Birmingham]] where he is the Head of School of Physics and Astronomy. His research focus is in the theory of correlated [[quantum]] systems, with current interests concerning: [[Non Fermi Liquid|Non Fermi liquids]], [[Quantum critical point|Quantum criticality]] and [[High-temperature superconductivity]].<ref>http://www.theory.bham.ac.uk/staff/schofield/research/</ref>

==Biography==
Andy Schofield was educated at [[Whitgift School]] before reading [[Natural Sciences]] at the [[Gonville and Caius College]], University of Cambridge. He did his PhD at the [[Cavendish Laboratory]] in Cambridge and won a College Research Fellowship at Gonville and Caius. He was a postdoctoral researcher at  [[Rutgers]], [[New Jersey]] before return to Cambridge as a [[Royal Society]] University Research Fellow.<ref>http://physics.aps.org/authors/a_j_schofield</ref> He joined the School of Physics and Astronomy at the University of Birmingham in 1999 and has been Head of School since 2010.

==Awards==
*The Schuldham Plate (1989), Gonville and Caius<ref>http://babylon.acad.cai.cam.ac.uk/students/prizes.php</ref>
*In 2002 Andy Schofield was awarded the [[Maxwell Medal and Prize]].<ref>http://www.iop.org/about/awards/career/maxwell/medallists/page_38660.html</ref> 
*He was elected Fellow of the [[Institute of Physics]] in 2002.
