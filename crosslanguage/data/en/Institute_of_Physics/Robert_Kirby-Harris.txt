'''Bob Kirby-Harris''' (born 12 June 1952) is the Secretary General of the [[International Union of Pure and Applied Physics]] and a former Chief Executive of the [[Institute of Physics]].<ref>[http://www.iop.org/publications/iop/2010/page_43881.html Promoting physics, supporting physicists] ''[[Institute of Physics]]''</ref>

==Early life==
He went to Ashford Grammar School (now [[The Norton Knatchbull School]]). He gained a 1st Class [[Bachelor of Science|BSc]] in Theoretical Physics in 1973 from the [[University of Kent]]. From [[Clare College, Cambridge]], he gained an [[Master of Arts (Oxbridge and Dublin)|MA]] in Applied Maths and Theoretical Physics in 1974. From the [[University of Sussex]] he gained a [[Postgraduate Certificate in Education|PGCE]] (Secondary schools) in 1975. From [[University of Plymouth|Plymouth Polytechnic]] he gained a [[Post Graduate Diploma in Management Studies|DMS]] <ref>[http://www.plymouth.ac.uk/courses/postgraduate/taught/2046/Diploma+in+Management+Studies+(Dms) DMS]</ref> in 1985. From [[Lancaster University]] he gained a [[Doctor of Philosophy|PhD]] in Higher Education Policy in 2003.

==Career==
===School teacher===
He was a school teacher from 1975-7 at Wakeford School (now called [[Havant Academy]]) in [[Havant]], [[Hampshire]].

===Royal Navy===
In 1977 he joined the [[Royal Navy]] becoming an instructor and a Lieutenant Commander in 1982. From 1977-9 he was a lecturer in Maths and Electronics at the Royal Navy Weapons Engineering School at [[Fareham]] ([[HMS Collingwood (establishment)|HMS Collingwood]]). From 1980-3 he was based at [[Chatham Dockyard|Chatham]], and from 1983-5 he was a Senior Maths Lecturer at the [[Royal Naval Engineering College]] (RNEC [[Manadon]]) at [[Plymouth]].

===Academic administration===
He has previous held posts as university manager at [[Middlesex University]] and the [[University of Namibia]].<ref>http://www.timeshighereducation.co.uk/story.asp?storyCode=194140&sectioncode=26 'I'm looking forward to making this a memorable year for physics'</ref>

Since 2008 he has been the Secretary General of the [[International Union of Pure and Applied Physics]]. He was Chief Executive of the Institute of Physics between 2005 and 2012.

==Personal life==
In 1979 he married Abigail Mee and they have two sons.
