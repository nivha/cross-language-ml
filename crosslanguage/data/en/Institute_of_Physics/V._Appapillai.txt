'''Velupillai Appapillai''' (13 November 1913 &ndash; 4 July 2001) was a [[Sri Lanka]]n physicist and academic. He was the dean of the Faculty of Science at the [[University of Ceylon]], Peradeniya.

==Early life and family==
Appapillai was born on 13 November 1913 in [[Batticaloa]] in eastern [[Ceylon]].<ref name=ST101099>{{cite news|last=Kumaravadivel|first=R.|title=Honouring this mighty man|url=http://www.sundaytimes.lk/991010/plus14.html|newspaper=[[The Sunday Times (Sri Lanka)]]|date=10 October 1999}}</ref> He was educated at [[Jaffna Central College]], graduating in 1931.<ref name=ST101099/> After school he joined [[Ceylon University College]], Colombo, graduating with a [[Bachelor of Science|BSc]] degree in physics.<ref name=ST101099/>

==Career==
In 1936 Appapillai was appointed a demonstrator in physics at the Ceylon University College.<ref name=ST101099/> He became a lecturer of physics in 1938.<ref name=ST101099/> He carried out his doctoral research in [[Colombo]] before going to the [[University of Manchester]] in 1946 to work under [[P. M. S. Blackett]], [[Langworthy Professor|Langworthy Professor of Physics]].<ref name=ST101099/> Appapillai's work on the composition and origin of cosmic rays at sea level in Colombo earned him a [[Doctor of Philosophy|PhD]] from the [[University of London]].<ref name=ST101099/>

After returning to Ceylon Appapillai collaborated with Blackett, [[Arnold Wolfendale]] and [[A. W. Mailvaganam]] in establishing that Associated Penetrating Particles did not exist within the limits of measurement.<ref name=ST101099/><ref>{{cite web|last=Sri Kantha|first=Sachi|title=Sri Lankan Tamil Scientists: An Inventory of Their Productivity|url=http://www.sangam.org/2009/03/Scientists.php|publisher=Ilankai Tamil Sangam|date=4 March 2009}}</ref> He was asked in 1963 to organise and develop a new Department of Physics at the [[University of Ceylon]], Peradeniya.<ref name=ST101099/> He was dean of the Faculty of Science at the university between 1970 and 1979 when he retired.<ref name=ST101099/>

Appapillai was a fellow of the [[Institute of Physics]] and a [[Chartered Physicist]].<ref name=ST101099/> He was a member of the Ceylon Association for the Advancement of Science, Pan Indian Ocean Science Association (PIOSA), [[UNESCO]] National Commission, Ceylon Standards Advisory Council and the National Science Council of Ceylon Standards.<ref name=ST101099/> He was the PIOSA's delegate at various international science conferences in the 1950s and 1960s.<ref name=ST101099/> In 1958 he was an observer at the [[British nuclear tests at Maralinga]] in Australia.<ref name=ST101099/>

==Later life==
The [[University of Peradeniya]] appointed Appapillai an [[emeritus professor]] in 1979 and awarded him an [[Honoris Causa|honorary DSc degree]] in 1984.<ref name=ST101099/> The [[University of Jaffna]] awarded him an honorary DSc degree in October 1999.<ref name=ST101099/> Appapillai headed a committee appointed by the [[University Grants Commission (Sri Lanka)|University Grants Commission]] which recommended the establishment of the [[Batticaloa University College]].<ref>{{cite web|title=History, Faculty of Arts & Culture, Eastern University, Sri Lanka|url=http://www.esn.ac.lk/FAC/history.html|publisher=[[Eastern University, Sri Lanka]]}}</ref> The University of Peradeniya has named one of its annual prizes after Appapillai.<ref>{{cite web|title=Prizes and Scholarships|url=http://www.pdn.ac.lk/sci/physics/priAndSchol.htm|publisher=[[University of Peradeniya]]}}</ref> 

Appapillai moved to the USA to live with his daughter.<ref name=ST101099/> He died on 4 July 2001 in [[Connecticut]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Velupillai Appapillai (1913 - 2001)|url=http://www.ancientfaces.com/person/velupillai-appapillai/7764750|publisher=Ancient Faces}}</ref>
