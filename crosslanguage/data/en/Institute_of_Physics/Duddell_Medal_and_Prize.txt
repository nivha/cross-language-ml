The '''Duddell Medal and Prize''' was a prize awarded annually by the [[Institute of Physics]] in the memory of [[William du Bois Duddell]], the inventor of the [[Electromagnetism|electromagnetic]] [[oscillograph]].  The medal was instituted by the Council of The Physical Society in 1923.  Between 1961 and 1973 the prize was awarded in alternate years. In 2008 the award was renamed the Gabor Medal and Prize, the first winner of the new award was Doreen Stoneham.
