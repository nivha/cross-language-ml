==Education==
Gleeson graduated from the [[University of Manchester]] in 1983 with a [[Bachelor of Science]] degree in [[Mathematics]] and [[Physics]].<ref name="wes">{{cite web|url=http://www.wes.org.uk/content/helen-gleeson|title=Helen Gleeson &#124; Women's Engineering Society|publisher=wes.org.uk|accessdate=2014-06-12}}</ref> She went to to study for a [[Doctor of Philosophy|PhD]] which she was awarded in 1986 for investigations of the optical and electro-optical properties of chiral [[mesophase]]s.<ref name="phd">{{cite thesis |degree=PhD |first=Helen|last=Gleeson |title=Optical and electro-optical properties of chiral mesophases |publisher=University of Manchester |date=1986 |url=http://ethos.bl.uk/OrderDetails.do?uin=uk.bl.ethos.383374|authorlink=Helen Gleeson}}</ref>

==Research==
