{{POV|date=July 2014}}'''Brian Cantor''' [[Order of the British Empire|CBE]] (born 11 January 1948) has been the [[Vice-Chancellor]] of the [[University of Bradford]] since October 2013.<ref name=uBradford>
[http://www.brad.ac.uk/about/news/our-new-vice-chancellor/ University of Bradford: announcing the appointment]</ref> Prior to this appointment he was Vice-Chancellor at the [[University of York]] from 2002 to 2013.<ref name=THE24April2013>
[http://www.timeshighereducation.co.uk/news/york-v-c-brian-cantor-to-join-bradford/2003454.article Times Higher: Brian Cantor joins Bradford]</ref> He is acknowledged as a world authority on materials manufacturing<ref name=Debretts>[http://www.debretts.com/people/biographies/search/results/4111/Brian+CANTOR.aspx Debrett's People of Today] Brian Cantor</ref> and is a former Vice-President of the [[Royal Academy of Engineering]].<ref name=UoB>[http://www.bradford.ac.uk/about/management/smt/professor-brian-cantor/] University of Bradford 5 June 2014</ref> In taking up the position in Bradford, he replaced [[Mark Cleary (professor)|Professor Mark Cleary]].<ref name=newvc>[http://www.brad.ac.uk/about/news/our-new-vice-chancellor/?bnr Professor Brian Cantor appointed as our new Vice-Chancellor] 24 April 2013</ref>

Cantor studied at [[Manchester Grammar School]] and [[Christ's College, Cambridge]]. He has worked at universities including Sussex, Oxford and York, and for companies including Alcan, Elsevier, General Electric and Rolls-Royce. He has advised organisations such as the UK Treasury, EPSRC, NASA, the EU and the Dutch, Spanish and German governments. He was a member of the Sainsbury review of UK science and innovation. He has chaired and been on the board of many companies and agencies, including Isis Innovation, the Kobe Institute, the UK Universities Pensions Forum,<ref name=EPF>[http://www.employerspensionsforum.co.uk/en/news/index.cfm/epfchair EPF appoints a new Chair] 7 December 2010</ref> the [[White Rose University Consortium]],<ref name=WRose>[http://www.whiterose.ac.uk/structure/executive_board White Rose Consortium Executive Board] 8 September</ref> Yorkshire Innovation, Leeds and York Economic Partnerships,<ref name=LYEP>[http://newsfeed.leedsvirtualnewsroom.co.uk/2011/01/board-members-announced-for-leeds-local.html Leeds and York Economic Partnership] 31 January 2011</ref> the Chambers of Commerce,<ref name=Chamber>[http://www.leedschamber.co.uk/index.php/your-board.html The Chamber, Leeds, York and North Yorkshire] 8 September 2011</ref> the National Science Learning Centre, the Centre for Low Carbon Futures,<ref name=CLCF>[http://www2.hull.ac.uk/news_and_events-1/news_archive/2009_news_archive/october/centre_for_low_carbon_futures.aspx Centre for Low Carbon Futures] 2 October 2009</ref> Science City York<ref name=SCY>[http://scy.co.uk/board Board of Science City York] 8 September 2011</ref> and the Worldwide Universities Network.<ref name=WUN>[http://www.onesource.com/free/Brian-Cantor/People/Profile/47420635-2 Worldwide Universities Network] 8 September</ref> He founded the Begbroke Science Park at Oxford and led the Â£1bn Heslington East campus development at York.<ref name=HesEast>[http://www.york.ac.uk/campus-development/expansion/ Heslington East Campus Expansion] 8 September 2011</ref>

Cantor was appointed [[Commander of the Order of the British Empire]] (CBE) in the 2013 New Year Honours for services to higher education.<ref>{{LondonGazette |issue=60367 |date=29 December 2012 |startpage=7 |supp=yes }}</ref>

==Research==

Cantor's research has investigated the manufacture of materials and has contributed to fundamental scientific advances as well as improvements in many industrial products. He has supervised over 130 research students and postdocs, published over 300 papers and books, given over 100 invited talks in more than 15 countries, and is on the ISI List of Most Cited Researchers.<ref name=ISI>[http://hcr3.webofknowledge.com/author.cgi?&link1=Browse&link2=Results&id=2804 Brian Cantor - ISI Entry] 8 September 2011</ref> He was awarded the Rosenhain and Platinum<ref name=IoMplatinum>[http://www.iom3.org/content/past-winners Brian Cantor - Platinum Medal IoM] 8 September 2011</ref> Medals of the Institute of Materials, Minerals and Mining. He is an Honorary Professor at Shenyang, Zhejiang and Nanjing<ref name=Nanjing>[http://www.york.ac.uk/news-and-events/news/2008/china-links/ York strengthens academic links with China] 1 April 2008</ref> Universities, an Honorary Member of the [[Indian Institute of Metals]],<ref name=IIM>[http://www.sciencenewsline.com/nature/2010111500009693.html Indian Honour for York's Vice-Chancellor] 15 November 2010</ref> a Member of [[Academia Europaea]]<ref name=AoE>[http://www.ae-info.org/ae/User/Cantor_Brian Academy of Europe entry] 8 September 2011</ref> and the [[World Technology Network]], and a Fellow of the [[Institute of Materials, Minerals and Mining]], the [[Institute of Physics]], the [[Chartered Management Institute]] and the [[Royal Academy of Engineering]]. He is a member of the Scientific Council at the [[IMDEA]] Materials Institute.
