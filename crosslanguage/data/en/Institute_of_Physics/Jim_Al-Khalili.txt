'''Jim Al-Khalili''' [[Order of the British Empire|OBE]] (born 20 September 1962) is an [[Iraq]]i-born [[United Kingdom|British]] [[theoretical physicist]], author and [[broadcasting|broadcaster]]. He is currently Professor of [[Theoretical Physics]] and Chair in the Public Engagement in Science at the [[University of Surrey]]. He has hosted several BBC productions about science and is a frequent commentator about science in other British media. In 2014 Al-Khalili was named as a RISE (Recognising Inspirational Scientists and Engineers) leader by the UK’s [[Engineering and Physical Sciences Research Council]] (EPSRC).<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.epsrc.ac.uk/newsevents/news/2014/Pages/riseawards.aspx/ |title= RISE AWARDS ANNOUNCED |date=31 March 2014 |accessdate= 8 April 2014}}</ref><ref name="twitter">{{twitter|jimalkhalili}}</ref><ref name="scopus"/>

He has been President of the [[British Humanist Association]] since January 2013.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://humanism.org.uk/2012/12/14/jim-al-khalili-named-president-elect-of-british-humanist-association/ |work= British Humanist Association |title= Jim Al-Khalili named President-elect of British Humanist Association |date=14 December 2012 |accessdate= 2 November 2013}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Born in [[Baghdad]] in 1962 to an [[Iraq]]i father and an English mother, Al-Khalili settled permanently in the UK in 1979.<ref name="whoswho"/> After completing his A-levels in 1982, he studied physics at the [[University of Surrey]] and graduated with a [[Bachelor of Science]] degree in 1986. Despite having a job lined up at the [[National Physical Laboratory (United Kingdom)|National Physical Laboratory]], he stayed on at Surrey to pursue a [[Doctor of Philosophy]] degree in nuclear reaction theory, which he obtained in 1989.<ref name="jimphd">{{cite thesis |degree=PhD |first=Jameel|last=Al-Khalili |title=Intermediate Energy Deuteron Elastic Scattering from Nuclei in a Three-Body Model |publisher=University of Surrey |date=1989 |url=http://ethos.bl.uk/OrderDetails.do?uin=uk.bl.ethos.328117|authorlink=Jim Al-Khalili}}</ref>

==Career in physics==
[[File:Jim Al-Khalili QED.jpg|thumbnail|Jim Al-Khalili talking about determinism at QED 2011]]
In 1989, Al-Khalili was awarded a [[Science and Engineering Research Council]] (SERC) postdoctoral fellowship at [[University College London]], after which he returned to Surrey in 1991, first as a research assistant, then as a lecturer.<ref>{{cite web|title=University of Surrey: Professor Jim Al-Khalili|url=http://www.surrey.ac.uk/physics/people/jim_al-khalili/|accessdate=2 November 2013}}</ref> In 1994, Al-Khalili was awarded an [[Engineering and Physical Sciences Research Council|Engineering and Physical Sciences Research Council (EPSRC)]] Advanced Research Fellowship for five years,<ref name="epsrc"/> during which time he established himself as a leading expert on mathematical models of exotic atomic nuclei. He has published widely in his field.<ref>{{cite doi|10.1103/PhysRevC.57.1846|noedit}}</ref><ref>{{cite doi|10.1016/S0375-9474(97)00111-5|noedit}}</ref><ref>{{cite doi|10.1103/PhysRevLett.76.3903|noedit}}</ref><ref>{{cite doi|10.1103/PhysRevC.54.1843|noedit}}</ref><ref>{{cite doi|10.1103/PhysRevLett.79.2771|noedit}}</ref><ref name="googlescholar">{{GoogleScholar|V9EAlmIAAAAJ}}</ref>

Al-Khalili is now a professor of physics at the [[University of Surrey]], where he also holds a chair in the Public Engagement in Science.<ref>{{cite web|title=Professor Jim Al-Khalili OBE, theoretical physicist, University of Surrey|url=http://www.theguardian.com/open-weekend/professor-jim-al-khalili-obe|accessdate=2 November 2013}}</ref> He has been a Trustee (2006-2012) and Vice President (2008-2011) of the [[British Science Association]].<ref>{{cite web |url= http://royalsociety.org/people/jim-al-khalili/ |title= Professor Jim Al-Khalili OBE |work= Royal Society |accessdate= 2 November 2013}}</ref> He also held an [[Engineering and Physical Sciences Research Council|EPSRC]] Senior Media Fellowship.<ref name="epsrc">{{cite book |url= http://books.google.co.uk/books?id=UGYe300kyXUC&lpg=PT74&ots=g08Uy32A8B&dq=Engineering%20and%20Physical%20Sciences%20Research%20Council%20Al-Khalili&pg=PT74#v=onepage&q&f=false |author= Laura Bowater |title= Science Communication: A Practical Guide for Scientists |year=2012 |page= 74}}</ref>

Al-Khalili was awarded the [[Royal Society]] [[Michael Faraday Prize]] for science communication for 2007<ref>{{cite news |last=Al-Khalili |first=Jim |url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/commentisfree/2008/jan/30/religion.world |title=The Arabic Science That Prefigured Newton |work=[[The Guardian]] |location=England |date=21 January 2008 |accessdate=17 March 2009 }}</ref> and elected an Honorary Fellow of the [[British Association for the Advancement of Science]]. He has been a Fellow of the [[Institute of Physics]] since 2000, when he also received the Institute's Public Awareness of Physics Award.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.thersa.org/large-text/events/speakers-archive/a/jim-al-khalili |title= RSA - Jim Al-Khalili |accessdate= 2 November 2013 |work= [[Royal Society of Arts]]}}</ref> He has lectured widely both in the UK and around the world, particularly for the [[British Council]]. He is currently a member of the [[British Council]] Science and Engineering Advisory Group,<ref>{{cite web|title=Biographies of Science and Engineering Advisory Group members|url=http://www.britishcouncil.org/new/PageFiles/4487/Science_advisory_groups181109.pdf|accessdate=2 November 2013}}</ref> a member of the [[Royal Society]] Equality and Diversity Panel,<ref>{{cite web|first=The Royal Society|title=Equality and Diversity Advisory Network|url=http://royalsociety.org/about-us/governance/committees/equality-diversity/|accessdate=2 November 2013}}</ref> an external examiner for the [[Open University]] Department of Physics and Astronomy, a member of the Editorial Board for the open access Journal PMC Physics A, and Associate Editor of Advanced Science Letters. He is also a member of the Advisory Committee for the [[Cheltenham Science Festival]]. In 2007, he was a judge on the BBC [[Samuel Johnson Prize]]<ref>{{cite web|title=The Samuel Johnson Prize for Non-Fiction: Judges Announcement|url=http://www.thesamueljohnsonprize.co.uk/node/54|accessdate=2 November 2013}}</ref> for non-fiction and has been a celebrity judge at the National Science & Engineering Competition Finals at The Big Bang Fair. He was appointed [[Officer of the Order of the British Empire]] (OBE) in the 2008 Birthday Honours.<ref>{{cite journal |journal=London Gazette |issue=58729 |date=14 June 2008 |page=9}}</ref> In 2013 he was awarded an Honorary Degree (DSc) from the University of London.<ref name="rhul"/>

== Science broadcasting ==
As a broadcaster, Al-Khalili is frequently on television and radio and also writes articles for the British press.<ref name="imdb">{{IMDb name|2629380}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.journalisted.com/jim-alkhalili |title= Jim Al-Khalili |work= Journalisted |accessdate= 2 November 2013}}</ref> In 2004, he co-presented the [[Channel 4]] documentary ''The Riddle of Einstein's Brain'', produced by ''[[Icon Films]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=C4 to unlock secrets of Einstein's brain|url=http://www.broadcastnow.co.uk/c4-to-unlock-secrets-of-einsteins-brain/1094951.article|accessdate=2 November 2013}}</ref> His big break as a presenter came in 2007 with ''Atom'', a three-part series on [[BBC Four]] about the history of our understanding of the atom and atomic physics.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://rationalist.org.uk/articles/4070/facing-the-future-an-interview-with-jim-al-khalili |title= Facing the future: an interview with Jim Al-Khalili |date= 7 March 2013 |accessdate= 2 November 2013 |first= Caspar |last= Melville |work= Rationalist Association}}</ref> This was followed by a special archive edition of ''[[BBC Horizon]]'', ''The Big Bang''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Lost Horizons: The Big Bang|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b00dcbqm|accessdate=2 November 2013}}</ref> In early 2009, he presented the BBC Four three part series ''[[Science and Islam (documentary)|Science and Islam]]'' about the leap in scientific knowledge that took place in the Islamic world between the 8th and 14th centuries.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.theguardian.com/science/blog/audio/2009/jan/12/science-weekly-podcast |title= Science Weekly: What has the Islamic world ever done for science? |date= 12 January 2009 |accessdate= 2 November 2013 |first= Alok |last= Jha |work= The Guardian}}</ref> He has contributed to programmes ranging from ''[[Tomorrow's World]]'', [[BBC Four]]'s ''Mind Games'', ''[[The South Bank Show]]'' to [[BBC One]]'s ''[[Bang Goes the Theory]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Bang Goes the Theory: Top five weird physics facts|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p0070lzn|accessdate=2 November 2013}}</ref> In October 2011, he began a programme on famous contemporary scientists on [[Radio Four]], called "[[The Life Scientific]]".<ref name="The Life Scientific">{{cite web|title=The Life Scientific|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/radio4/features/science-discovery/jim-al-khalili/#intro|accessdate=2 November 2013}}</ref> The first of this series featured his interview with [[Sir Paul Nurse]].<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.surrey.ac.uk/physics/news/stories/2011/66047_jim_alkhalilis_new_science_series_starts_on_radio_4.htm |title= Jim Al-Khalili's new science series starts on Radio 4 |date= 7 October 2011 |accessdate= 2 November 2013 |work= University of Surrey}}</ref>

In 2004, Al-Khalili was chosen as one of twenty-one "Faces of UK Science" for an exhibition in London's [[National Portrait Gallery (United Kingdom)|National Portrait Gallery]].<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.york.ac.uk/news-and-events/news/2012/events/paradox-lecture/ |title= Focus falls on greatest enigmas of science |date= 30 April 2012 |accessdate= 2 November 2013 |work= The University of York}}</ref>

In 2010, Al-Khalili presented a feature on the ''[[Doctor Who]]'' [[DVD]] of ''[[The Time Monster]]'', entitled ''Between Now... and Now!'', discussing the science behind time travel in that particular story.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.purpleville.pwp.blueyonder.co.uk/rtwebsite/MythsAndLegends.htm |title=Doctor Who Restoration Team: Myths And Legends}}</ref> He presented a three-part BBC Four series on the history of chemistry called ''[[Chemistry: A Volatile History]]'', which was nominated for a [[BAFTA]] award, as well as a documentary on chaos theory called ''The Secret Life of Chaos''. He is also one of several presenters on ''[[Genius of Britain]]'', a five-part series for Channel 4 shown in 2010, along with [[Stephen Hawking|Prof. Stephen Hawking]], [[Richard Dawkins|Prof. Richard Dawkins]], [[Sir James Dyson]] and [[David Attenborough|Sir David Attenborough]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Genius of Britain|url=http://www.channel4.com/programmes/genius-of-britain/episode-guide|accessdate=2 November 2013}}</ref>

Al-Khalili is a regular guest on [[BBC Radio 4]]'s ''[[In Our Time (BBC Radio 4)|In Our Time]]'', presented by [[Melvyn Bragg]].<ref> He has been a guest on the following ''In Our Time'' episodes: [http://www.bbc.co.uk/radio4/history/inourtime/inourtime_20040219.shtml Rutherford], [http://www.bbc.co.uk/radio4/history/inourtime/inourtime_20041118.shtml Higgs Boson], [http://www.bbc.co.uk/radio4/history/inourtime/inourtime_20051124.shtml The Graviton], [http://www.bbc.co.uk/radio4/history/inourtime/inourtime_20081218.shtml The Physics of Time], [http://www.bbc.co.uk/radio4/history/inourtime/inourtime_20091112.shtml The Discovery of Radiation].</ref> He has been a guest on ''[[Start The Week]]''<ref>{{cite web|title=Start the week|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/radio4/factual/starttheweek_20070723.shtml|accessdate=2 November 2013}}</ref> and the ''[[Today (BBC Radio 4)|Today]]'' programme. In April, 2009, he presented a three-part series called ''The Secret Scientists'' for the [[BBC World Service]].<ref>{{cite web|title=The Secret Scientists|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/worldservice/documentaries/2009/04/090414_secret_scientists.shtml|accessdate=2 November 2013}}</ref> He was the ''[[Desert Island Discs]]'' guest on 14 February 2010.

Al-Khalili also hosts a regular "Jim meets..." interview series at the [[University of Surrey]], which is published on the university's [[YouTube]] channel. Guests have included [[David Attenborough|Sir David Attenborough]], [[Robert Winston|Lord (Robert) Winston]], [[Brian Cox (physicist)|Professor Brian Cox]] and [[Rowan Williams]], [[Archbishop of Canterbury]].<ref>{{YouTube|u=UniversityofSurrey|The University of Surrey}}</ref>

In 2011, Al-Khalili hosted a three-part documentary series on BBC Four entitled [[Shock and Awe: The Story of Electricity]].<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p00kjqcv |title= BBC Four - Shock and Awe: The Story of Electricity |accessdate= 2 November 2013}}</ref>

In January 2012, Al-Khalili presented a [[Horizon (BBC TV series)|Horizon]] special on [[BBC 2]], which examined the latest scientific developments in the quest to discover the [[Higgs Boson]], with preliminary results from the [[Large Hadron Collider]] experiment at CERN suggesting that the elusive particle does indeed exist. In October that same year, he started presenting "The Life Scientific" on [[BBC Radio Four]],<ref name="The Life Scientific"/> a programme in which celebrated living scientists are interviewed; the first was [[Sir Paul Nurse]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b015n3b7|title=BBC iPlayer - The Life Scientific:Paul Nurse|accessdate=2012-05-10}}</ref> he has since interviewed a series of notable scientists, including [[Richard Dawkins]], [[James Lovelock]], [[Steven Pinker]], [[Martin Rees]], [[Jocelyn Bell Burnell]], [[Mark Walport]] and [[Tim Hunt]].

==Personal life==
Al-Khalili lives in [[Southsea]] with his wife Julie. They have a son, Dave, and a daughter, Kate, both at university. Al-Khalili describes himself as an [[atheism|atheist]],<ref>{{cite AV media |quote="I find it more comfortable to say I'm an atheist, and for that I probably have someone like Dawkins to thank." |people= Jim Al-Khalili |url= http://www.bbc.co.uk/radio4/features/science-discovery/jim-al-khalili/#intro |title= BBC Radio 4 - Science Explorer: Jim Al-Khalili featured in The Life Scientific}}</ref> remarking, "as the son of a [[Protestantism|Protestant]] Christian mother and a [[Shia Islam|Shia]] [[Muslim]] father, I have nevertheless ended up without a religious bone in my body".<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.guardian.co.uk/commentisfree/2008/jan/30/religion.world |title= It's time to herald the Arabic science that prefigured Darwin and Newton |work= The Guardian |date= 30 January 2008 |accessdate= 19 February 2010}}</ref> Al-Khalili has been a supporter of [[Leeds United]] football club since the [[Don Revie|Revie]] days of the early 1970s.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://humanism.org.uk/about/our-people/distinguished-supporters/professor-jim-al-khalili-obe/ |title= Professor Jim Al-Khalili OBE |accessdate= 2 November 2013 |work= British Humanist Association}}</ref>
