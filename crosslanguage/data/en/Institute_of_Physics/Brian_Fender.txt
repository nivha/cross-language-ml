'''Sir Brian Edward Frederick Fender''' (15 September 1934-)<ref>{{cite news|title=Obituaries: Birthdays|date=2009-09-15|work=Guardian Obituary Pages|publisher=The Guardian|pages=37|accessdate=25 January 2010}}</ref><ref name = "WWW">[http://www.ukwhoswho.com/view/article/oupww/whoswho/U15604 ''FENDER, Sir Brian (Edward Frederick)'', Who's Who 2012, A & C Black, 2012; online edn, Oxford University Press, Dec 2011; online edn, Nov 2011, accessed 27 Oct 2012]</ref> is an English academic executive.

==Career==
He was Chief Executive of the [[Higher Education Funding Council for England]] from 1995-2001.<ref name="Indy">{{cite news|title=Education: Howard's Way; Stylish and Open; The University Funding Council's new boss wants public debate. Will this be good for higher education?|last=Hodges|first=Lucy|date=2001-10-18|publisher=The Independent|pages=Education, 9|accessdate=25 January 2010}}</ref> Prior to that he was [[Vice-Chancellor]] of [[Keele University]] (1985â€“95),<ref name="Indy"/> Associate Director and Director of the [[Institut Laue-Langevin]] in [[Grenoble]], France and Chairman of the Science Board of the UK's [[Science and Engineering Research Council]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Appointments|date=1990-09-25|publisher=The Times|accessdate=25 January 2010}}</ref> He is a graduate and Fellow of [[Imperial College]].<ref name="IKT-bio">{{cite web |url=http://www.ikt.org.uk/boardmember/SirBrianFenderCMGMInstKT-24.aspx |title=IKT - Institute of Knowledge Transfer - Executive Committee - Sir Brian Fender CMG MInstKT |publisher=www.ikt.org.uk |accessdate=2010-01-25 }}</ref>

Sir Brian is currently a member of the University Grants Committee, Hong Kong, President of the [[National Foundation for Educational Research]], Chairman of the National Council for Drama Training and a Director of Higher Aims Ltd, a private consultancy involved in higher education and research management. Sir Brian is a Fellow of the [[Institute of Physics]], the [[Royal Society of Chemistry]] and a Companion of the [[Chartered Management Institute]]. He has honorary degrees or fellowships from eleven universities and colleges.<ref name="IKT-bio"/>

Sir Brian is the current Chairman and President of the [[Institute of Knowledge Transfer]].<ref name="IKT-bio"/>

==Early life==
Fender was born in [[Barrow-in-Furness]], and lived in the [[River Eden, Cumbria|Eden]] Valley until 1949.<ref>{{cite web | title =Review of higher education in Cumbria | work =News | publisher =Higher Education Funding Council for England | date =15 May 2003 | url =http://www.hefce.ac.uk/news/hefce/2003/cumbria.htm | accessdate =9 February 2012 }}</ref> He attended [[Carlisle Grammar School]] and [[Sale Grammar School|Sale County Grammar School]].<ref name=TLS>{{cite web | last =Swain | first =Harriet |author2=Williams, Lynne | title =Brian Fender | work =Times Higher Education | publisher =TSL Education | date =5 March 1999 | url =http://www.timeshighereducation.co.uk/story.asp?storyCode=145335&sectioncode=26 | accessdate =9 February 2012 }}</ref>

==Personal life==
Fender has been married twice, and has a son and three daughters.<ref name=TLS/>
