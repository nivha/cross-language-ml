'''S. Samar Hasnain''' is the [[Max Perutz]] professor of [[Molecular Biophysics]] at the [[University of Liverpool]]. He is current [[editor in chief]] for the [[Journal of Applied Crystallography]]. In 1991 he became a [[Fellow of the Institute of Physics]] and in 2002 he became a [[Fellow of the Royal Society of Chemistry]]. Additionally, in 1997 he became a [[Third World Academy of Sciences|Fellow of the Third World Academy of Sciences]] and in 2011 a [[Fellow of the Society of Biology]]. He is also currently  editor-in-chief for the [[Journal of Synchrotron Radiation]].<ref name=crystal>

==Background==
In 1970 Hasnain earned a Bachelor of Science (Honours) an M.Sc. in 1972 and a Ph.D. in 1977. His research areas include [[neurodegenerative disease]]s, methodical [[drug discovery]], [[protein crystallography]], and [[synchrotron radiation]].<ref name=univliv>
