'''Robert Bellamy Clifton''' (1836â€“1921) was a [[United Kingdom|British]] scientist.<ref>{{cite journal |author=Royal Astronomical Society |title=Obituaries |journal=[[Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society]] |volume=LXXXII |pages=248}}</ref>

==Academic career==

Clifton was educated at [[University College, London]] and [[St Johns College, Cambridge]] where he studied under [[Sir George Stokes]].<ref>{{cite journal|author=Cahan,David | title=Helmholtz and the British scientific elite: From force conservation to energy conservation|journal=Notes Rec. R. Soc.|doi=10.1098/rsnr.2011.0044}}</ref>
In 1860 he went to [[Owens College, Manchester]] as Professor of [[Natural Philosophy]]. In 1865 he was appointed Professor of experimental Natural Philosophy at [[Oxford University]]. While at Oxford he designed [[Clarendon Laboratory]] and gave research space to [[Charles Vernon Boys]].
<ref>{{cite book|author= Gooday, Graeme and Fox, Robert (editors) |title= Physics in Oxford, 1839-1939|publisher= Oxford University Press|year=1998 |isbn=9780198567929 | doi=10.1093/acprof:oso/9780198567929.001.0001}}</ref> 
<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.physics.ox.ac.uk/history.asp?page=HistoryLong|title=Physics at the university of Oxford|publisher=oxford university|accessdate = 29 September 2012}}</ref>
On 04/06/1868 he became a fellow of the [[Royal Society]].
He was president of the [[Physical Society]] (now [[Institute of Physics]]) from 1882 until 1884. Clifton retired from Oxford in 1915.

==Family==
Clifton's father was the clergyman [[Robert Cox Clifton]]. His daughter Catharine Edith was married to the surgeon [[Henry Souttar]].
