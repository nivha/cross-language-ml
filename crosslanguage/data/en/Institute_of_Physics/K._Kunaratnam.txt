Professor '''Kanthia Kunaratnam''' is a [[Sri Lankan Tamil]] physicist, academic and former [[Vice-Chancellor of the University of Jaffna|vice-chancellor]] of the [[University of Jaffna]].

==Early life==
Kunaratnam joined the [[University of Ceylon]], Colombo in 1954 and graduated in 1958 with a first class honours [[Bachelor of Science|BSc]] degree in physics.<ref name=KK>{{cite web|title=Prof.K.Kunaratnam|url=http://www.jfn.ac.lk/sci/phy/staffdetail.php?action=show&name=Kunaratnam&id=Kunaratnam5|publisher=Department of Physics, [[University of Jaffna]]}}</ref>

==Career==
After university Kunaratnam worked at the University of Ceylon, Colombo and University of Ceylon, Peradeniya.<ref name=KK/> He studied at [[Imperial College London]] between October 1960 and July 1963, obtaining a [[Diploma of Imperial College|DIC]] and a [[Doctor of Philosophy|PhD]] degree.<ref name=KK/> 

Kunaratnam was visiting fellow and lecturer in geophysics at the Department of Geology, [[University of Durham]] between January 1971 and September 1971.<ref name=KK/> He was visiting fellow at the Department of Physics, [[Queen Elizabeth College]] from October 1971 to December 1971.<ref name=KK/>

Kunaratnam was appointed Founder Head of the Department of Physics at the Jaffna Campus of the [[University of Sri Lanka]] in April 1975.<ref name=DoP>{{cite web|title=History|url=http://www.phy.jfn.ac.lk/about.php|publisher=Department of Physics, [[University of Jaffna]]}}</ref> He remained head of the department until October 1980.<ref name=DoP/> He was visiting lecturer at the School of Physics, [[University of Science, Malaysia]] between Jan 1981 and October 1981.<ref name=KK/> He then returned to the University of Jaffna and resumed being Head of the Department of Physics, serving until February 1985.<ref name=DoP/> He was visiting fellow and professor at the Department of Geophysics, [[University of Edinburgh]] between October 1988 and August 1989.<ref name=KK/> He was visiting fellow at the [[Department of Earth Sciences, University of Cambridge|Department of Earth Sciences]], Bullard Laboratories, [[University of Cambridge]] between Sept 1989 and October 1989.<ref name=KK/>

Kunaratnam was Head of the Department of Computer Science at the university between January 1991 and November 1993.<ref>{{cite web|title=Heads of the Department|url=http://www.csc.jfn.ac.lk/index.php/about|publisher=Department of Computer Science, [[University of Jaffna]]}}</ref> He then served as Head of the Department of Physics from March 1994 and April 1994.<ref name=DoP/>

Kunaratnam served as dean of the [[Faculty of Science, University of Jaffna|Faculty of Science]] twice, from January 1977 to May 1978 and January 1985 to March 1988.<ref>{{cite web|title=Dean's Office|url=http://www.jfn.ac.lk/sci/deansOffice.html|publisher=[[Faculty of Science, University of Jaffna|Faculty of Science]], [[University of Jaffna]]}}</ref> He served as vice-chancellor of the university between April 1994 and February 1997.<ref>{{cite web|title=Establishment of the Jaffna Campus of the University of Sri Lanka|url=http://www.jfn.ac.lk/aboutusmore.htm|publisher=[[University of Jaffna]]|archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20080322160808/http://www.jfn.ac.lk/aboutusmore.htm|archivedate=22 March 2008}}</ref> Kunaratnam retired in April 2000.<ref name=KK/> He is currently serving as [[emeritus professor]] at the Department of Physics.<ref>{{cite web|title=Academic Staff|url=http://www.jfn.ac.lk/sci/phy/staff.php?action=academic1|publisher=Department of Physics, [[University of Jaffna]]}}</ref>

Kunaratnam was elected fellow of the [[Royal Astronomical Society]] in 1998 and fellow and chartered Physicist of the [[Institute of Physics]] in 1999.<ref name=KK/> He was awarded an [[honorary doctorate]] from the University of Jaffna in 2001.<ref name=KK/>
