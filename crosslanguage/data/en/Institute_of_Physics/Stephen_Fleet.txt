'''Stephen George Fleet''' (September 28, 1936 â€“ May 18, 2006) was a Master of [[Downing College, Cambridge]], the [[Cambridge University]] [[Registrary]] and a researcher in [[Mineralogy|mineral sciences]] and [[crystallography]]. 

Stephen Fleet was educated at [[Brentwood School (England)|Brentwood School, Essex]], [[Lewes Priory School|Lewes County Grammar School, Sussex]] and [[St John's College, Cambridge]], where he received his [[doctor of philosophy|doctorate]]. His research fields were the crystal structure of minerals, particularly phase transformations in minerals and meteorites.

In 1963 Fleet moved to [[Fitzwilliam College, Cambridge|Fitzwilliam House]] and was a founding [[fellow]] when Fitzwilliam achieved collegiate status in 1966. In 1974 he moved to Downing College as [[bursar]] and fellow and later served the college as Vice-Master (1985&ndash;1987, 1991&ndash;1994 and 1997&ndash;2000) and as Master from 2000 to 2003. Between 1983 and 1997 Fleet was the [[Registrary|University Registrary]], the chief administrative officer of the university.

He died from [[cancer]] at the [[Hammersmith Hospital]], London on 18 May 2006.
