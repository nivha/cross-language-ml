'''John Fothergill''', BSc, MSc, PhD, CEng, (born 1953 Malta) is Pro-Vice-Chancellor of [[City University London]]. He was formerly Head of Engineering, Dean of Science and Pro-Vice-Chancellor at the [[University of Leicester]]. He is a Fellow of the [[IEEE]],<ref>{{cite web | url=http://services27.ieee.org/fellowsdirectory/getdetailprofile.html?custNum=aJbw%2FiBtMfnv9KtNUqliUg%3D%3D | title=IEEE Fellows Directory - Member Profile | accessdate=21 May 2014}}</ref> the [[Institution of Electrical Engineers|IEE]], and the [[Institute of Physics]].<ref>{{cite news | url=http://www.iop.org/interactions/archive/INTJuly11Digital.pdf | title=New Fellows | work=Interactions (Institute of Physics) | date=July 2011 | accessdate=21 May 2014}}</ref>

He has over 120 refereed papers and over 20 postgraduate students <ref>http://www2.le.ac.uk/departments/engineering/people/academic-staff/john-fothergill</ref>

==History==
[[File:John Fothergill sailing Squib at Rutland Water.jpg|thumb|alt=John Fothergill]]John Charles Fothergill was born in [[Malta]]. He lived in [[London]] and in [[Fish Hoek]] [[South Africa]] before settling in [[Iver|Iver Heath]] at the age of 10.  He attended Slough Grammar School (now [[Upton Court Grammar School]]) and won prizes in Music and Mathematics. He graduated from the University College of North Wales (now [[Bangor University]]) with a BSc (Hons) in Electronic Engineering (1975), an MSc in Electronic Materials and Devices (1976), and a PhD in the Dielectric Properties of Biopolymers (1980) which he studied with Profs Ron Pethig and John Lewis.

He worked at the [[Standard Telecommunication Laboratories]] from 1979 to 1984 as a Senior Research Engineer on high-voltage power cables.

In 1984, he moved to the University of Leicester as a lecturer. He was promoted to professor in 2000 and was Head of the Electrical Power and Power Electronics Research Group, Dean of Science, Pro Vice-Chancellor (Learning and Teaching), and Head of the [http://www2.le.ac.uk/departments/engineering Department of Engineering].  He remains an Honorary Visiting Professor at the University of Leicester.

In 2012, he became Pro Vice-Chancellor (Research and Enterprise) at [[City University London]].

He is a visiting professor at the [http://www.ctgu.edu.cn/# China Three Gorges University] and [http://www.tyut.edu.cn/newsite2/english/ Taiyuan University of Technology], China.
