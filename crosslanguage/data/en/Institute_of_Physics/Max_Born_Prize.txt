The '''Max Born Prize''' is a scientific prize awarded yearly by the [[Deutsche Physikalische Gesellschaft|German Physical Society]] (DPG) and the British [[Institute of Physics]] (IOP) in memory of the German physicist [[Max Born]]. The terms of the award are that it is "to be presented for outstanding contributions to [[physics]]". The award goes to physicists based in Germany and in the UK or Ireland in alternate years.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/about/awards/bilateral/born/page_38469.html |title=The Born medal and prize |publisher=[[Institute of Physics]] |accessdate=August 30, 2011}}</ref><ref name="DPG_Born_Prize">{{cite web|url=http://www.dpg-physik.de/preise/preistraeger_mb.html |title=Max-Born-Preis |language=German |trans_title=Max Born Prize |publisher=[[Deutsche Physikalische Gesellschaft|German Physical Society]] |accessdate=August 30, 2011}}</ref>

The prize is accompanied by a silver medal "about 6 cm in diameter and 0,5 cm thick. One face carries a profile of Max Born and his name and dates. The other face carries the equation pq - qp = h/2πi and the full names of IOP and DPG. The recipient's full name and year of award is engraved around the rim."<ref name="DPG_Statute">{{cite web|url=http://www.dpg-physik.de/preise/satzungen/born.html|title=Statute of the Max-Born-Prize|publisher=[[Deutsche Physikalische Gesellschaft|German Physical Society]]|accessdate=October 23, 2012}}</ref>
