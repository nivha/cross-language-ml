}}'''Prof. Dame Julia Mary Goodfellow''', [[Order of the British Empire|DBE]], [[FMedSci]], [[Institute of Physics|FInstP]] (née Lansdall; born 1 July 1951) is [[Vice-Chancellor]] of the [[University of Kent]],<ref>[http://www.kent.ac.uk/news/stories/dbeforvc/2010 University of Kent website, 1]</ref><ref>[http://www.kent.ac.uk/news/inthenews/june/24.html University of Kent website, 2]</ref><ref>[http://www.bbk.ac.uk/about_us/fellows/goodfellow Birkbeck College website]</ref><ref name="microsoft">{{AcademicSearch|53319377}}</ref> and Chair of the [[British Science Association]].<ref>[http://www.britishscienceassociation.org/british-science-association/council British Science Association Council]</ref>

==Early life and education==
Goodfellow completed her undergraduate degree in physics at the [[University of Bristol]] and completed a Ph.D. in biophysics at the [[Open University]]<ref name="goodfellowphd">{{cite thesis |degree=PhD |first=Julia|last=Goodfellow |title=Structural studies of the corneal stroma |publisher=Open University |date=1975 |url=http://ethos.bl.uk/OrderDetails.do?uin=uk.bl.ethos.456973|authorlink=Julia Goodfellow}}</ref> Research Unit in [[Oxford]]. She began her career as Professor of Biomolecular Science at [[Birkbeck College]] in 1995.<ref>[http://www.kent.ac.uk/vicechancellor/profile.html Goodfellow profile at the University of Kent website]</ref><ref>{{cite pmid|17052110}}</ref><ref>{{cite pmid|15840832}}</ref><ref>{{cite pmid|12854956}}</ref><ref>{{cite pmid|12668424}}</ref><ref>{{cite pmid|11897357}}</ref><ref>{{cite pmid|11839282}}</ref><ref>{{cite pmid|11706012}}</ref> In 2002, she was appointed CEO of the [[Biotechnology and Biological Sciences Research Council]], which funds scientific research projects at British universities.
