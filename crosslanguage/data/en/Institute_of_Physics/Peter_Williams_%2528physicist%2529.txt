'''Peter Michael Williams''' (born 22 March 1945),<ref name=Birth>{{cite web|title=Birthday's today|url= http://www.telegraph.co.uk/archive/2012-3-22.html |publisher=The Telegraph|accessdate=20 April 2014|date=22 March 2012|quote=Sir Peter Williams, Chancellor, University of Leicester, 2005–10; Master of St Catherine's College, Oxford, 2000–2002, 67 }}</ref> [[Order of the British Empire|CBE]], [[Fellow of the Royal Academy of Engineering|FREng]], [[Fellow of the Royal Society|FRS]] is a [[United Kingdom|British]] [[physicist]].

Williams completed his [[undergraduate degree|first degree]] and [[PhD]] with thesis titled ''Defect structure and luminescent properties of semiconductors'' at the [[University of Cambridge]], and began an academic career at [[Selwyn College, Cambridge|Selwyn College]]. He then moved to industry and worked first at VG Instruments and later [[Oxford Instruments]]. He was chairman of Oxford Instruments from 1991 until his retirement in 1999.

Sir Peter is currently Chairman of the [[National Physical Laboratory, UK|National Physical Laboratory]] and Vice President and Treasurer of the [[Royal Society]]. He was previously Master of [[St Catherine's College, Oxford]] (2000&ndash;2002), President of the [[Institute of Physics]] (2000&ndash;2002), President of the [[British Association for the Advancement of Science]] (2002&ndash;2003) and Chairman of the UK's [[Engineering and Technology Board]] (2001&ndash;2006).<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.etechb.co.uk/uploads/Catalyst%2025.pdf | title = Sir Gareth Roberts appointed as chairman | format = PDF | accessdate = 2007-02-08 }}</ref>  He has been a member of the [[InterAcademy Council|Interacademy Council]]'s Committee to Review the [[Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change]] that reported in August 2010.   

He received the CBE in 1992 and was [[knight bachelor|knighted]] in the [[Queen's Birthday Honours]] list of 1998. He was elected a [[fellow of the Royal Society]] in May 1999.

In 2005 Williams became the fifth [[chancellor (education)|chancellor]] of the [[University of Leicester]]. He was also appointed patron for research at [[Marie Curie Cancer Care]]. He retired from this position in 2010.
