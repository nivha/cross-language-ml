Professor Sir '''John Edwin Enderby''' [[CBE]], [[Fellow of the Royal Society|FRS]] is a British [[physicist]], and was Professor of Physics at [[University of Bristol]] from 1976 to 1996.<ref>[http://www.bristol.ac.uk/pace/graduation/honorary-degrees/hondeg06/enderby.html Biography]</ref>

==Life==
He was the H.O. Wills Professor of Physics and Head of Department, from 1981 to 1994.
He was Deputy-Adjoint of the [[Institut Laueâ€“Langevin]] from 1965 to 1988. He was elected a [[Fellow of the Royal Society]] in 1985 and was Physical Secretary and Vice President of the society from 1999 to 2004.<ref>http://www.vrijmetselaarsgilde.eu/Maconnieke%20Encyclopedie/RMAP~1/TRS/manage.htm</ref> He was President of the [[Institute of Physics]] from 2004 to 2006.  Sir John's contributions have been recognised by the award of a CBE in 1997 and a Knighthood for services to Science and Technology in 2004.
