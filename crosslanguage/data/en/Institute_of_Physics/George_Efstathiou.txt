'''George Petros Efstathiou''' [[Fellow of the Royal Society|FRS]] (born 2 September 1955) is a British [[astrophysicist]] who is Professor of Astrophysics and Director of the [[The Kavli Foundation#Astrophysics|Kavli Institute for Cosmology]] at the [[University of Cambridge]]. He was previously [[Savilian Professor of Astronomy]] at the [[University of Oxford]].

==Life==
Efstathiou was educated at [[Tottenham Grammar School]] which he left at age 16 and to which he returned as a lab technician. He then  studied at [[Keble College, Oxford]] and the [[University of Durham]]. He was a research assistant in the Astronomy Department of [[University of California Berkeley]] from 1979 to 1980, then moved to the [[Institute of Astronomy, Cambridge|Institute of Astronomy]] at the [[University of Cambridge]], holding research [[Oxbridge Fellow|fellowships]] at [[King's College, Cambridge]] from 1980 to 1988. He was appointed  as [[Savilian Professor of Astronomy]] at the [[University of Oxford]] in 1988 (a post that he held in conjunction with a fellowship at [[New College, Oxford]])
and served as Head of Astrophysics between 1988 and 1994. He returned to Cambridge in 1997 as Professor of Astrophysics (1909) and a Fellow of King's College.  Efstathiou served as Director of the Institute of
Astronomy between 2004 and 2008.He became the first Director of the [[Kavli Institute for Cosmology]] in 2008.<ref name=Who>{{cite web|title=Efstathiou, Prof. George Petros|work=[[Who's Who|Who's Who 2010]]|publisher=[[Oxford University Press]]|date=November 2009|url=http://www.ukwhoswho.com/view/article/oupww/whoswho/U14741|accessdate=3 March 2010}}</ref>

==Awards and honours==
He was awarded the [[Maxwell Medal and Prize]] of the [[Institute of Physics]] in 1990, and appointed a [[Fellow of the Royal Society]] in 1994, the same year in which he was awarded the Bodossaki Foundation Academic and Cultural Prize for Astrophysics. Other awards in the Robinson Prize in Cosmology ([[Newcastle University|University of Newcastle]], 1997) and the [[Dannie Heineman Prize for Astrophysics]] ([[American Institute of Physics]] and [[American Astronomical Society]], 2005).<ref name=Who/> He received the [[Gruber Prize in Cosmology]] for 2011 jointly with [[Marc Davis (astronomer)|Marc Davis]], [[Carlos Frenk]] and [[Simon White]].
