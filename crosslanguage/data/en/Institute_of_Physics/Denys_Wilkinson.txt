'''Sir Denys Haigh Wilkinson''' [[Fellow of the Royal Society|FRS]] (born 5 September 1922 in [[Leeds]], [[Yorkshire]]) is a [[UK|British]] [[nuclear physicist]].  He was educated at [[Loughborough Grammar School]] and [[Jesus College, Cambridge|Jesus College]], [[Cambridge University|Cambridge]]. He holds the higher degree of ScD, an HonFilDr degree and an HonLLD degree.  Wilkinson is the inventor of the Wilkinson [[Analog-to-Digital Converter]] (ADC), a fundamental electronic device with wide application.

He was elected a [[Fellow of the Royal Society]] in 1956 and he won the [[Hughes Medal]] in 1965 and the [[Royal Medal]] in 1980. Sir Denys has been an Honorary Fellow of Jesus College,  Cambridge, since 1961. From 1976 to 1987 he was Vice-Chancellor of the [[University of Sussex]] (appointing him Emeritus Professor of Physics in 1987). He was [[Knight Bachelor|knighted]] in 1974.<ref>{{LondonGazette|issue=46430|startpage=12745|date=13 December 1974|accessdate=5 May 2009}}</ref>

In 2001 the [[Nuclear Physics Laboratory]] at the [[University of Oxford]], which he helped to create, was renamed as the [[Denys Wilkinson Building]] in his honour.<ref>[http://www.physics.ox.ac.uk/pp/dwb/dwb.htm Oxford Physics â€“ Denys Wilkinson Building]</ref>
