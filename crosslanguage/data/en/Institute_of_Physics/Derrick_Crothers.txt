'''Derrick Crothers''' (born 24 June 1942) is a [[Northern Ireland|Northern Irish]] [[mathematician]], [[physicist]] and former [[politician]].

Crothers grew up in [[Cookstown]] and studied at [[Rainey Endowed School]], where he won the top mathematics and Science State Exhibition prize in 1959.  He then read mathematics at [[Balliol College]], Oxford, before obtaining a doctorate from the [[Queen's University of Belfast]] in 1966.<ref name="inelastic">Derrick Crothers and J. V. Mullan, "Inelastic heavy particle collisions", ''Science Progress'' (1995), p.35</ref>  In the following years, he lectured at Queen's, undertook research at [[University College London]], worked as a tutor for the [[Open University]], and was elected as a Fellow of the [[Institute of Physics]].<ref name="strute">Karl Strute, ''Who's Who in Technology'' (1984), p.157</ref>

At the [[Northern Ireland Assembly election, 1973]], Crothers was elected for the [[Alliance Party of Northern Ireland]] in [[South Antrim (Assembly constituency)|South Antrim]],<ref name="strute" /> receiving 5,975 first preference votes.  He did not stand for the [[Northern Ireland Constitutional Convention]] or subsequent assemblies, but did contest the Lower Falls area of [[Belfast City Council]] at the [[Northern Ireland local elections, 1989|1989 local elections]].  He took only 135 votes and was not elected.<ref>[http://www.ark.ac.uk/elections/85-89lgbelfast.htm Local Government Elections 1985 - 1989: Belfast], Northern Ireland Elections</ref>

In 1985, Crothers was promoted to a Personal Chair in Theoretical Physics at Queen's.<ref name="inelastic" />  He was elected Member of the [[Royal Irish Academy]] in 1991 and Fellow of the [[American Physical Society]] in 1994, before retiring in 2007.<ref>[http://www.qub.ac.uk/schools/SchoolofMathematicsandPhysics/Staff/StaffInfo/?staffid=6 Prof Derrick Crothers], [[Queen's University Belfast]]</ref>
